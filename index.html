At RV Public School (RVPS), learning begins with questions, not answers. From Pre-Nursery to Grade 10, students are taught to engage with what they learn not just remember it. Lessons move through discussion, simple research and hands-on work, so children learn to observe, ask questions and gradually make sense of the world around them.

The focus is on helping students think for themselves. They learn to understand information and use it with clarity in real life. Over time, this builds their confidence and the ability to approach problems with a steady, independent mindset.

From the Principal

Principal's Message

"

At RVPS, education begins with curiosity and is shaped through consistent engagement. In the early years, learning works best when there is a strong connection between school and home. Parent–student engagement is therefore not treated as an add-on, but as an essential part of the process. When children feel that continuity, they settle in better, gain confidence, and show a natural willingness to learn.

As students grow older, the focus gradually shifts. They are expected to express themselves clearly, take part in discussions, and handle ideas with a certain level of maturity. Opportunities to build communication and public speaking are built into their routine including through the SkillSphere collaboration.

The academic approach remains steady and considered. There is no attempt to rush through content. Time is given for students to understand what they are learning and work through it properly. They are expected to question, to make sense of what they study, and not rely only on memorised responses.

The aim of RVPS is simple. To help students grow into individuals who are confident in their thinking, responsible in their actions and prepared for what lies ahead.

Principal, RVPS School · Bengaluru
M.Ed.  ·  B.Ed.  ·  15+ years in progressive school leadership

Our Approach

How Students Learn at RVPS

Curiosity-Driven Learning

Students research real-world topics like scientific phenomena, global mysteries and questions like the Bermuda Triangle and flight disappearances; encouraging deeper thinking and the habit of independent inquiry from an early age.

Robotics & AI From Grade 3

Students begin learning robotics and AI concepts early, helping them understand how technology actually works and building future-ready skills well ahead of peers.

Financial Literacy for Students

Students learn the basics of money, value and economic thinking including classroom barter activities that make abstract concepts tangible and practical.

Digital Minimalism

RVPS promotes balanced technology use, where students learn to think deeply and focus without excessive screen dependence, a genuinely rare and valuable skill today.

Creativity Integrated With Subjects

Art and craft activities are linked to academic subjects so students understand ideas through visual and hands-on learning.

Classroom Barter System

Through fun classroom barter activities among students, students understand the value of exchange and acquire basic negotiation and decision-making skills from an early age.

Learning Responsibility — The Notebook Initiative

Unused notebook pages are donated to Youth for Seva, where they are repurposed into notebooks for children in need. Through this initiative, students experience sustainability and social responsibility as lived practice.

Questions Parents Ask

Everything you need to make a confident decision. If your question isn't here, call or write to us, we're happy to answer.

RVPS is affiliated with the Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) board, administered by the Council for the Indian School Certificate Examinations (CISCE). The ICSE board is known for its strong emphasis on English language proficiency, analytical thinking, project-based learning, and conceptual depth — all of which align directly with RVPS's inquiry-based approach to education.
School hours are Monday to Friday: 8:00 AM – 3:30 PM and Saturday: 8:00 AM – 12:30 PM. Campus gates open at 7:45 AM. The academic year follows the Karnataka school calendar; typically beginning in June and closing in March, with major breaks during Dasara, Christmas, New Year and summer. A detailed academic calendar is shared at the time of admission.
Fee details are shared transparently during the campus visit or on request. RVPS does not charge any capitation fee or hidden charges, all fees are collected through official, receipted channels only. The fee structure is designed to reflect the quality of education while remaining fair. Please contact us or visit the campus to receive the complete fee schedule for your child's grade.
Yes, school transport is available covering key areas across Bengaluru. All transport vehicles are fitted with GPS tracking and accompanied by a trained attendant, particularly for younger students. Transport route availability and fee details can be confirmed at the time of admission based on your home location. Contact our admissions team with your area and we will check route availability for you.
RVPS maintains a deliberately low student to teacher ratio. Smaller class sizes mean each child receives more direct attention, faster feedback, and a stronger sense of belonging in the classroom. This is one of the meaningful advantages of joining RVPS, where batches are intentionally kept small. Specific ratios by grade are shared during the campus visit.
Yes, RVPS has a school uniform. It is practical, comfortable and suited to Bengaluru's climate. Uniform details including where to purchase are shared at admission confirmation. There is no requirement to arrange the uniform before you complete the admission process.
Students participate in sports, music, art and technology-related activities alongside their academics. Robotics workshops, science exhibitions and art displays are part of the regular school calendar. The SkillSphere programme, RVPS's dedicated public speaking and debate strand, runs from Grade 3 upwards as a core co-curricular activity.
RVPS teachers are qualified, experienced educators trained in the ICSE curriculum and in inquiry-based teaching methodology. They are trained to guide students through discussion and research-based learning while maintaining strong academic rigour. Teachers also participate in regular professional development to stay current with modern pedagogy and best classroom practices.
No formal documents are needed for the campus visit, come as you are. Bringing your child along is welcome so they can experience the environment for themselves. Jot down any questions you have for the principal or admissions team beforehand.
